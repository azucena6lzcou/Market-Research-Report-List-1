<p><p>Sunscreens typically contain two main types of ingredients: physical and chemical. Physical sunscreen ingredients, like zinc oxide and titanium dioxide, create a barrier on the skin that reflects UV rays. In contrast, chemical sunscreen ingredients, such as avobenzone and octisalate, absorb UV radiation and convert it into heat. Other market ingredients may include moisturizers, antioxidants, and skin-soothing agents, enhancing skin health while providing sun protection. Each type addresses different user preferences and skin types, ensuring varied options for sun safety.</p></p>
