<p><ul><li>Cold Soak</li><li>Hot Dip</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The nutritional wine market can be categorized into two main types: Cold Soak and Hot Dip. Cold Soak involves fermenting grapes at lower temperatures, which enhances the extraction of color and flavor while preserving delicate aromas. This method is often used in producing high-quality wines. On the other hand, Hot Dip entails immersing grapes in hot water or steam before fermentation, promoting a quicker extraction of phenolic compounds and sugars. Both methods cater to diverse consumer preferences for flavor profiles and health benefits.</p></p>
